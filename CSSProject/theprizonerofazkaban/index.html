Story Summary

In "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban" (Part 3), Harry returns to Hogwarts for his third year, but his summer is marked by disturbing news: Sirius Black, a notorious prisoner, has escaped from Azkaban, and it’s believed he’s coming for Harry. The school is placed under guard by Dementors, terrifying creatures that drain happiness. Despite their presence, Harry learns more about his parents' past and the betrayal that led to their deaths. As the year progresses, Harry discovers secrets about Sirius Black’s connection to his family. With the help of Hermione's time-turner, they uncover the truth—that Sirius is not the enemy but was framed for murder. The real traitor is Peter Pettigrew, a former friend of Harry’s parents, who has been hiding in plain sight as Ron’s pet rat, Scabbers. Harry and Hermione free Sirius, who reveals that he is Harry’s godfather. However, Pettigrew escapes, preventing Sirius from clearing his name. The story ends with Harry feeling hopeful for the future, knowing he has someone who truly cares for him. This installment is pivotal for Harry, as it introduces the theme of betrayal and deepens his understanding of his parents’ legacy, shaping his journey forward.
